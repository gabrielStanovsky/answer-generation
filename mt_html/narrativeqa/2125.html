Task Instruction:

In this task, you are given a passage, a question, a correct answer, and a predicted answer. The task is to score how closely a predicted answer captures the same information as a correct answer using the passage, question, and human commonsense knowledge.

Scoring is on a scale from 1 to 5 .
A score of 1 means the predicted answer captures none of the information of the correct answer, and thus is a completely wrong answer.
A score of 5 means the predicted answer captures the same information as the correct answer, and thus is a completely right answer.

Each HIT will contain multiple questions and predictions for the same context so it needs to only be read once per HIT.


Note: A predicted answer may be a correct answer, but to be given a score of 5, it must be equivalent to the correct answer. An example is provided below of this case.

Note: The score you give should not depend on the capitalization of the correct and predicted answer.

Note: A helpful tip for searching for the correct answer in the passage is using Control+F to search for it.

Examples:

Passage: …He goes to prison to serve out a five years' sentence, but after three years have passed is pardoned out through the efforts of Maria Wyndham, Mr. Fletcher's granddaughter, whom he has loved for years.…
Question: What is the relationship of Maria Wyndham to Mr. Fletcher?
Correct Answer: grandaughter

Predicted Answer: Maria is Mr. Fletcher's granddaughter.
Score: 5
Justification: The predicted answer captures the same information as the correct answer when taking into account the passage and question.

Predicted Answer: maria is maria 's granddaughter
Score: 1
Justification: While the prediction has the word granddaughter, it has additional information that makes it nonsensical (one cannnot be their own granddaughter).

Passage: …On the way she sees the minister, Mr. Miles, and her friend Liff Hyatt. They are on their way to the mountain because Charity’s mother is dying. When they arrive, Charity’s mother is already dead, and the three of them bury her.…
Question: What happened to Charity's mother?
Correct Answer: Charity's mother dies.

Predicted Answer: She dies.
Score: 5
Justification: The predicted answer captures the same information as the correct answer when taking into account the passage and question.

Predicted Answer: she is dying
Score: 3
Justification: While this is technically a correct answer, it is not exactly the same as the correct answer. However, it is not completely different from the correct answer.

Passage: …The next day, two men, John and Jim (who are drug dealers), arrive at the apartment to pick up the package. Peter and Michael mistakenly give Mary to them instead, and shortly after they leave, Peter discovers the actual package.…
Question: Who comes to pick up the package the next day ?
Correct Answer: Drug Dealers

Predicted Answer: two men
Score: 4
Justification: In the passage, the two men are the same people as the drug dealers. However, two men could be any two people (for example, Peter and Michael), so we will say that it isn't completely equivalent.

Predicted Answer: the drug drug dealers
Score: 4
Justification: The predicted answer is correct, though slightly ungrammatical, so we give it a 4.

Predicted Answer: John, Jim, and Peter
Score: 3
Justification: "John, Jim" would be a 5, however the prediction includes Peter, who is not a drug dealer. We therefore consider this answer half correct.

Predicted Answer: Peter and Michael
Score: 1
Justification: Completely wrong names of the drug dealers.
